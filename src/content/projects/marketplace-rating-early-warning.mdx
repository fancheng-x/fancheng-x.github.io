## Executive summary

On a two-sided review marketplace, rating is the product. When a venue's rating starts to slide,
the platform loses trust and the operator loses demand — but by the time the average moves, the
damage is done. I led a team building an **early-warning system** that forecasts a 7-day rating
drop for individual restaurants and, just as importantly, explains the operational reasons behind
the risk. It scores **5,393 restaurants** from **100,000+ reviews** with an AUC of **0.75** and
recall of **0.66**, turning a lagging metric into a leading, actionable signal.

## Business context

A star rating is a slow-moving average — which makes it a terrible alarm. A venue can accumulate a
week of angry reviews and still show four stars, so operators and the platform react only after the
number visibly falls and recovery is expensive. The decision we wanted to support: *which venues are
at risk right now, and what should someone do about it?*

## The core decision

- **Triage** — which restaurants are most likely to see a rating drop in the next 7 days?
- **Root cause** — what's driving the risk (service, wait times, food quality, cleanliness)?
- **Action** — what signal do we hand an operator or account manager so they can intervene before the rating moves?

## My role

I led problem framing, feature strategy, and the modeling approach, and owned how the output was
framed as an operational signal rather than a raw probability. I coordinated the team across data
prep, modeling, and the LLM-based complaint analysis.

## Analytical approach

- **Feature engineering (29 features).** Time-series signals (rating trajectory, review velocity),
  behavioral signals (reviewer patterns), and **sentiment** extracted from review text.
- **Modeling.** A Random Forest classifier tuned for recall on the imbalanced "drop" class —
  because missing an at-risk venue is costlier than a false alarm.
- **Explainability (SHAP).** Every prediction comes with the features driving it, so the signal is
  interpretable, not a black box.
- **LLM complaint classification.** Llama 3.2 (via Ollama) categorizes free-text complaints into
  operational root causes — turning "what" (rating at risk) into "why" (e.g. slow service).

## Recommendation & solution

The output is an operator-facing signal: a ranked risk list plus the SHAP-driven and LLM-classified
reasons behind each flag, so intervention is targeted (fix the wait-time problem at *this* venue)
rather than generic. It reframes rating from a scoreboard into an early-warning dashboard.

## Business impact

The system converts a **lagging metric into a leading one**. Instead of reacting after a rating
falls, the platform and its operators get a prioritized, explained watchlist — protecting
marketplace quality where it's most at risk.

## Validation & limitations

Metrics are reported on held-out data; recall was deliberately favored over precision given the
asymmetric cost of a missed at-risk venue. The model reflects historical review behavior and would
need periodic retraining as platform and user behavior shift.

## What I'd do next

- Calibrate the intervention threshold to a real operator's review capacity.
- A/B test whether flagged-and-acted-on venues actually recover faster than a control group.
- Extend root-cause classification into suggested next actions, not just categories.

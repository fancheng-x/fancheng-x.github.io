## Executive summary

A serious job search is an operations problem: applications, contacts, coffee-chat recaps, and
follow-ups pile up across notebooks and Notion faster than anyone keeps them organized — and a
stale pipeline is useless for deciding what to do next. So I designed and built **Career CRM
Copilot**, a local, LLM-powered application (Streamlit + Claude) that turns that scattered data into
**structured relationship intelligence and recommended next actions**. It's a nine-page product I own
end to end — data model, UX, retrieval, and the safety design around an AI agent that can change your
data.

## The problem

Off-the-shelf trackers make *you* do the data entry and give you back a spreadsheet, not a decision.
The questions that actually matter in a search — *who haven't I followed up with? which applications
are stalling? what did that person say they cared about?* — require structure the tools don't build
and judgment they don't offer.

## What I built

A local-first app with nine pages, each mapped to a real job-search job-to-be-done:

- **Structured extraction.** Paste any free text — a note, a recap, a full job description — and the
  LLM decides what it is and pulls out contacts, companies, applications, a summary, and a follow-up
  draft. A pasted JD becomes a role you've applied to; a same-name contact is detected so you attach
  instead of duplicating.
- **A confirm-before-write action agent.** Plain-language commands ("set this contact to high
  priority", "log a coffee chat", "remind me to follow up") are resolved to *who* and *what* you
  mean — and turned into a **plan you approve before anything is written**. The agent never mutates
  data silently.
- **Hybrid search + rerank.** Semantic (embedding) similarity blended with keyword scoring across
  contacts, applications, and interactions, then LLM re-ranking that returns a ranked answer *with a
  relevance reason, an evidence quote, and a suggested action.*
- **A decision dashboard.** KPI cards, an **application funnel** (response rate, offer rate, average
  days in each stage), distribution charts, an **action center** (open tasks, high-priority contacts
  with no next action, pending follow-ups), and a one-click weekly decision memo.
- **Insights & follow-ups.** Summaries of recent activity — themes, positioning signals, gaps — and
  editable LinkedIn / email / message follow-up drafts.

## Product & UX decisions

The interesting work here was product, not just code:

- **Confirm-before-write.** An agent that can edit your records is only trustworthy if it can't
  surprise you. Every change is a reviewable plan first — a deliberate safety choice.
- **Local-first by design.** Everything lives in a local SQLite database and is never uploaded.
  Privacy wasn't a feature bolted on; it's the architecture.
- **Ambiguity handling.** When a name is ambiguous the agent asks rather than guesses, and the
  extractor detects same-name contacts to prevent duplicates.
- **Measuring AI quality.** Every correction I make on the review screen is logged as extraction
  feedback (accuracy / correction rate), so the system's reliability is *observed*, not assumed.

## Why it matters

This is the clearest single demonstration of what I like doing: **spotting a messy, ambiguous
workflow, modeling the data and decisions underneath it, and using AI to remove friction — while
taking the product responsibility that comes with an agent that can act.** It's product thinking,
operations thinking, and applied AI in one owned system.

## What I'd build next

- A retrieval-quality evaluation harness (faithfulness / answer-correctness) to put numbers on the
  search and extraction quality I currently track qualitatively.
- Fit-scoring of new roles against my own profile to auto-prioritize the pipeline.
- Calendar-aware follow-up nudges driven by the action center.

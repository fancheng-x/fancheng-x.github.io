## Executive summary

Fraud detection is a cost-tradeoff problem disguised as a modeling problem. Catch more fraud and you
also flag more legitimate applicants for costly manual review. I built a **LightGBM** identity-fraud
model on **1,000,000 financial-product applications** that makes that tradeoff explicit — and used it
to recommend a review threshold, not just report an accuracy number.

## The decision

Not "is this application fraud?" but **"where do we set the review cutoff so that prevented losses
outweigh investigation cost?"** — a business decision the model exists to inform.

## Approach

- Engineered **velocity, recency, and cross-linkage** features from identity attributes — the
  signals that expose fraud rings and synthetic identities.
- Trained a **LightGBM** classifier suited to a large, highly imbalanced dataset.
- Evaluated on **out-of-time** data to reflect real deployment, where tomorrow's fraud doesn't look
  exactly like yesterday's.
- Framed performance as a **cost curve** across review thresholds rather than a single score.

## Why it matters

The recommendation balanced prevention impact against false-positive investigation cost — the
difference between a model that scores well and one an operations team can actually run.

## Validation & limitations

Evaluated on out-of-time data to avoid over-optimistic estimates. The savings figure is a modeled
estimate under stated assumptions about loss and investigation cost, not a booked result.
